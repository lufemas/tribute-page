Miyamoto Musashi

Swordsman, philosopher, strategist, writer and rōnin

Since the precise dates are not known, the timeline will use the round Musashi's age of events mixed with some more accepted dates. Here's a time line of Miyamoto Musashi:

  • ( Probably: 1584) - Born in Miyamoto Bennosuke, Harima Province or Mimasaka Province, Japan
  • 7 years old - He was educated in Buddhism and basic skills such as writing and reading. His father trained him with the sword.
  • 13 years old - His first duel, defeated Arima Kihei, a sword adept of the Shinto ryū.
  • 1599 - Musashi left his village spending his time traveling and engaging in duels.
  • 1600 - He fought in a war against the Togugawa clan.
  • 21 years old - He went up to Kyōtō and fought duels with several adepts of the sword from famous schools, but he never lost.
  • 1605 to 1612 - He travelled extensively all over Japan in musha shugyō, a warrior pilgrimage during which he honed his skills with duels.
  • 1611 - Musashi began practicing zazen, a meditative discipline, at the Myōshin-ji temple.
  • 1612 - Musashi fought his duel with Sasaki Kojirō, who was known as "The Demon of the Western Provinces". The duel was short. Musashi killed his opponent with a bokken ( wooden training sword)
  • 1614 to 1615 - Musashi participated in the war between the Toyotomi and the Tokugawa.
  • 1615 - he entered the service of Ogasawara Tadanao of Harima Province, as a "Construction Supervisor". He helped construct Akashi Castle
  • 1621 - He helped to lay out the organisation of the town of Himeji. During this period he taught martial arts, specializing in instruction in the art of shuriken(ninja-star)-throwing. During this period of service, he adopted a son.
  • 1622 - Musashi's adoptive son, Miyamoto Mikinosuke, became a vassal to the Himeji Domain. Possibly this prompted Musashi to leave.
  • 1627 - Musashi began to travel again.
  • 1633 - Musashi began staying with Hosokawa Tadatoshi, daimyō of Kumamoto Castle.
  • 1641 - Musashi wrote a work called the Hyoho Sanju Go ("Thirty-five Instructions on Strategy") for Hosokawa Tadatoshi; this work overlapped and formed the basis for the later The Book of Five Rings.
  • 1643 - He retired to a cave named Reigandō as a hermit to write The Book of Five Rings.
  • 1645 - Musashi finishes The Book of Five Rings. Same year that he died on the Reigandō cave
